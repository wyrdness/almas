## Overview

The Almas (also Almasty) is a humanoid cryptid reported from the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Mongolia, the Caucasus, Pamir Mountains, and Altai Mountains. Described as standing 5-6.5 feet tall, covered in reddish-brown hair with a more human-like face than ape-like, featuring a pronounced brow ridge.

The creature is generally described as shy and avoiding human contact. The most famous case involves **Zana**, an alleged female Almas captured in the 1850s in Abkhazia who lived among humans and reportedly bore children with human features. DNA analysis in 2013 of Zana's descendants was inconclusive but suggested possible sub-Saharan African ancestry.

Some researchers theorize the Almas could represent surviving Neanderthal populations or other archaic humans adapted to Central Asian mountains.

## Notable Cases

### Zana: The Captured Almas (1850s-1880s) ⭐

> **Period**: 1850s-1880s
> **Location**: Ochamchire region, Abkhazia (Caucasus)
> **Witnesses**: Multiple villagers over decades
> **Evidence**: Historical accounts, descendants' DNA, skull examination

Zana was allegedly a female Almas captured in the Caucasus region in the 1850s. Described as covered in reddish-brown hair with more human-like features than ape-like. She was reportedly integrated into local society, lived in captivity, and bore several children with local men.

**Key Points**:
- Lived among humans for approximately 30 years
- Bore 4-6 children with local men
- Children exhibited some unusual features but integrated into society
- Died in the 1880s
- Investigated by Soviet researcher Boris Porshnev in 1960s
- Skull of one son examined, showed unusual features
- **2013 DNA Analysis**: Bryan Sykes analyzed DNA from descendants
  - Suggested possible sub-Saharan African ancestry
  - Did not match known ape species
  - Inconclusive regarding Almas hypothesis
  - Raises questions about African ancestry in 19th century Caucasus

**Credibility Assessment**: High — Most documented potential cryptid case. Multiple witnesses over extended period, physical descendants exist, DNA analysis conducted. However, DNA results suggest human (possibly African) origin rather than unknown species.

**Interpretations**:
1. Zana was an Almas (archaic human or unknown primate)
2. Zana was a human of African descent with unusual features who was ostracized
3. Zana was a human with genetic abnormalities or medical condition

## Theories & Explanations

### Cryptozoological/Scientific Theories

1. **Surviving Neanderthal Populations**
   Almas represents isolated surviving Neanderthal populations in remote Central Asian mountains. Human-like features, brow ridge, and location within Neanderthal historical range support this. Proposed by Boris Porshnev.

2. **Homo erectus Remnant**
   Could represent surviving Homo erectus or related archaic human species adapted to mountain environments.

3. **African Ancestry Hybrid** (based on Zana DNA)
   Possible descendant of displaced African population with unusual features. 2013 DNA analysis suggested sub-Saharan African ancestry, raising questions about historical population movements.

4. **Unknown Primate**
   Undiscovered primate species adapted to Central Asian mountains, though descriptions are more human-like than typical primates.

### Skeptical Explanations

1. **Folklore Tradition**
   Purely folkloric with no biological basis, part of widespread Central Asian wildman cultural traditions.

2. **Misidentification**
   Sightings are misidentifications of humans, bears, or other known animals in remote areas with limited contact.

3. **Zana as Human Outcast**
   Zana was human (possibly African) with unusual features who was ostracized or enslaved. DNA evidence supports human ancestry. Historical period coincides with slavery and displacement.

## The Neanderthal Question

The Almas phenomenon is unique among cryptids for its connection to legitimate paleoanthropology:

**Why Neanderthal Survival Theory Exists**:
1. **Geographic overlap**: Central Asia within Neanderthal historical range
2. **Physical description**: Brow ridge, human-like build match Neanderthal features
3. **Recent extinction**: Neanderthals survived until ~40,000 years ago (recently in evolutionary terms)
4. **Interbreeding**: Modern humans and Neanderthals interbred, showing compatibility
5. **Remote habitat**: Isolated mountain populations could theoretically persist

**Why It's Unlikely**:
1. **No physical specimen**: No bones or bodies found
2. **DNA evidence**: Zana's DNA suggests human, not archaic human
3. **Population genetics**: Isolated populations unlikely to survive millennia
4. **Lack of archaeological evidence**: No recent Neanderthal artifacts
